How to Change Dressing

1)         With clean hands, gently wash the wound with antibacterial soap and water using a clean washcloth.  Rinse well and pat dry with a clean towel.  You may begin showering with first dressing change.

2)         Scabs/crusts delay healing.  If they form, gently loosen them with a clean washcloth soaked with warm water.

3)         Apply a generous coat of petroleum jelly over sutures to keep them moist (very important).

4)         Cover wound with a Band-Aid, telfa pad, or gauze and tape.  Keep dressing dry between changes.

Complications That Can Occur

BLEEDING:  A small amount of bleeding can be expected.  Rarely, excessive bleeding may occur.  If this happens, use a clean gauze to apply firm CONTINUOUS pressure over the bandage for 10-15 minutes.  If this does not control the bleeding, continue to hold pressure and please call our office at (248) 344-2370 between the hours of 7:30AM and 4:00PM.  Wednesday hours are 8:30AM-5:00PM.  After 5:00 pm or on weekends, please call the dermatologist-on-call at (248) 981-2905 who may direct you to go to the nearest emergency room (not Urgent Care) to be checked. 

INFECTION:  Infection seldom occurs; however, if you notice any of the following symptoms, notify our office immediately:  A thick white/yellow drainage (pus) with foul odor, a very inflamed and swollen surgical site, fever or chills.  Swelling and bruising is expected after some surgeries. 

PAIN:  Usually patients experience only minimal pain in the surgical area.  Acetaminophen taken according to package directions should control the pain.  Patients with kidney or liver disease should check with their medical doctor before taking acetaminophen.   Avoid aspirin, ibuprofen products, and all anti-inflammatories, as they increase the risk of bleeding.

ACTIVITY: Strain on the wound can cause bleeding.  Do not bend at the waist and do not lift anything heavier than five pounds.  Please restrict your activities as discussed with your surgical team.

Who to Call?

If you have any questions or problems relating to your surgery, please call our office Monday through Friday at (248) 344-2370 and ask to speak to a nurse. If it is after 4:30 M, TU, TH, FRI or after 5:00 Wednesday or anytime on the weekend and you are having symptoms such as fever, chills, heavy bleeding, vomiting, or unusual discharge, please call (248) 981-2905 and ask for the dermatologist on call.
